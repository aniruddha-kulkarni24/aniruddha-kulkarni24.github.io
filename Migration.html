Migration

Many species migrate locally or over long distance to aviod adverse climatic comditation & in search of food. Hundreds of thousands of waterfowl migrate each winter to India from central & northern Asia , covering huge distances. smaller winter migrants include wagtails , warblers & bluethroats. The huge number of wintering birds also accounts for the large numbers of wintering ornithologists & bird enthusiasts who visit the subcontinent over these months.

Summer visitor are much fewer. The multi-coloured Indian Pitta is one such bird well worth searching out. It winters in south India & sri Lanka , & visits the deciduous forest & scrubland of the Himalayan foothills & the north-west around May , staying until it has breed.

Scientists have still to discover exactly how birds navigate during migration , although it is clear that they have a number of means to do so. Apart from sighting landmarks , it has been proved that birds make use of an internal magnetic compass & of the position of the sun & stars. It is also thought that sound & smell may play a role. The Siberian Crane is one which finds its way every year from Siberia to the same square kilometre of wetland in the Keoladeo Ghana National Park at Bharatpur in Rajasthan.
